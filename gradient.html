Unit 8. Gradient Descend excercise

In Machine Learning's Unit 8 there was a code provided, which demonstrated in a nutshell, how an essential neural networks mathematical tool -- gradient descend -- works. As a part of formative excercise, I played around with it and produced a group of curves representing different predefined learning rate (the value indicating the speed of the descend of each step).

See the code with commentary and outcomes.

As can be observed, interestingly, the bigger the descending step (variable "learning rate"), the slower increases its accuracy.

This situation can be practically depicted as a ball bouncing down the slope with a step long enough to overcome the local minimum and travel back up. By extending the step length, this way we quickly get out of stability.
